What you can learn

Pick an instrument or subject

Tap any subject to see how lessons are structured, what you'll cover and who it suits. Lessons are tailored to each learner — there's no rigid one-size-fits-all curriculum.

Piano

The instrument I've taught the longest. We build solid technique and reading from day one, but always around music you actually want to play — film themes, pop, classical or your own ideas.

  • Beginner to ABRSM Grade 8
  • Sight-reading, scales & aural built in gently
  • Acoustic, digital piano or keyboard all welcome

Best for: ages 6+, complete beginners, returning adults.

Guitar

Acoustic, classical or electric. Most students are strumming real songs within a few weeks, then we layer in fingerstyle, chords beyond the basics, and reading or tab depending on your goals.

  • Chords, strumming patterns & song-learning
  • Fingerstyle, scales & improvisation
  • Rock School & Trinity grades if wanted

Best for: ages 8+, teens, adult beginners.

Violin

Beautiful but tricky to start — so I focus heavily on posture, bow control and a lovely tone from the very first lessons, using plenty of duets so you're never playing alone.

  • Posture, bowing & intonation foundations
  • ABRSM grades & orchestral repertoire
  • Suitable from age 6 with a correctly sized instrument

Best for: ages 6+, school orchestra players, adults.

Singing

A warm, judgement-free space to find your voice. We work on breathing, pitch and confidence through songs you love, whether you're prepping for a show, an audition or simply singing for joy.

  • Breath support, pitch & vocal health
  • Musical theatre, pop & classical styles
  • Audition & performance preparation

Best for: ages 9+, nervous beginners, performers.

Music Theory

The grammar of music, taught so it actually makes sense. Essential for ABRSM Grade 6+ practical exams, GCSE and A-Level music, and genuinely useful for any musician who wants to understand what they're playing.

  • ABRSM Theory Grades 1–8
  • GCSE & A-Level music support
  • Online or in-studio sessions available

Best for: exam candidates, composers, GCSE/A-Level students.

Meet your teacher

Hello, I'm Eleanor

I'm a Reading-based music teacher with a degree in Music from the University of Surrey and 14 years of private teaching behind me. Before going full-time with tuition, I performed and accompanied across Berkshire and taught in two local primary schools.

My philosophy is simple: progress comes from enjoyment. When a student loves what they're learning, the practice takes care of itself. So I build every lesson around real music the student cares about, while quietly developing the technique, reading and theory that let them go further.
